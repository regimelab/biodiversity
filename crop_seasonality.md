California has something being harvested every month, but the “predominant” crops by month vary by region and market; there is no single official list that all sources agree on. The lists below synthesize common extension and seasonal‑produce calendars for California and should be taken as typical patterns, not strict limits.[3][4]

## January

- **Citrus**: Navel oranges, lemons, tangerines, grapefruits.[4][3]
- Winter vegetables: Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, celery, lettuce, carrots, beets, leafy greens.[3][4]
- Avocados and early strawberries in coastal areas.[3]

## February

- **Citrus** continues: Navel oranges, lemons, tangelos, tangerines, grapefruit.[4][3]
- Winter vegetables: Cauliflower, broccoli, cabbage, carrots, peas, artichokes, asparagus (early fields).[2][3]
- Strawberries begin to scale up along the coast.[4][3]

## March

- **Strawberries** become one of the most visible crops (Central Coast).[3][4]
- Citrus: Navel oranges, lemons, some grapefruits still active.[9][3]
- Spring vegetables: Asparagus, artichokes, peas, carrots, early squash.[2][3]

## April

- **Strawberries** peak and are widespread in markets.[4][3]
- Citrus transitions to Valencia oranges and grapefruit; lemons remain common.[9][3]
- Spring vegetables: Snap peas, beans, squash, carrots, beets, radishes.[2][3]

## May

- **Stone fruit starts**: Early peaches, apricots, some plums; table grapes begin in warmer areas.[9][3]
- Citrus: Valencia oranges, grapefruit, lemons still harvested.[9][3]
- Warm‑season vegetables: Sweet corn, beans, squash, cucumbers, onions, early tomatoes and potatoes.[3][9]

## June

- **Stone fruits and berries**: Peaches, plums, apricots, cherries (early), strawberries, raspberries, blackberries.[9][3]
- Grapes start in southern and interior valleys; citrus (Valencia, grapefruit, lemons) still present.[3][9]
- Summer vegetables: Sweet corn, tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, eggplant, potatoes.[4][3]

## July

- **Grapes and melons** expand and become major summer crops, especially in the Central Valley.[9][3]
- Peaches, plums, nectarines, apricots, figs, apples (early varieties) are widely harvested.[3][9]
- Vegetables: Sweet corn, tomatoes, beans, peppers, squash, onions, sweet potatoes.[4][3]

## August

- **Table grapes** and **melons** (cantaloupe, watermelon, others) are among the most prominent crops.[9][3]
- Stone fruits continue: Peaches, plums, nectarines; figs and apples are abundant.[3][9]
- Summer vegetables: Tomatoes, cucumbers, beans, squash, carrots, peppers, potatoes.[4][3]

## September

- **Grapes** (table, wine, raisins) dominate many regions, along with late melons.[7][3]
- Late peaches, plums, and apples; early pears and pomegranates begin.[9][3]
- Vegetables: Sweet corn, squash, cucumbers, beans, carrots, peppers, potatoes.[4][3]

## October

- **Grapes** (especially wine grapes) and **nuts** (almonds, walnuts, pistachios) are key fall crops, though nut harvest timing can span late summer into fall.[5][6]
- Apples, pears, figs, pomegranates, and persimmons (starting) are common.[3][9]
- Pumpkins and winter squash, plus continued tomatoes, peppers, carrots, sweet potatoes.[5][3]

## November

- **Tree nuts** remain important in many districts; late wine grapes and olives (for oil) are still harvested.[6][5]
- Fall fruit: Apples, pears, kiwifruit, persimmons, pomegranates, some late melons and citrus starting to ramp up.[9][3]
- Vegetables: Carrots, lettuce, brassicas (broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage), onions, sweet potatoes.[5][3]

## December

- **Citrus** becomes predominant again: Mandarins/tangerines, navel oranges, lemons, grapefruit.[4][3]
- Cool‑season vegetables: Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, lettuce, carrots, beets, leafy greens.[2][3]
- Kiwifruit, some apples, and macadamia nuts appear in smaller volumes.[5][3]
